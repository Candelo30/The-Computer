The Computer

The computer is a programmable digital electronic machine that executes a series of commands to process the input data, conveniently obtaining information that is then sent to the output units. A computer is made up of numerous and diverse integrated circuits and various support, extension and accessory components, which together can perform various tasks extremely quickly and under the control of a program (software).

Fundamental parts of a computer.

  • The computer case
  • Motherboard
  • (CPU) processor
  • Fan cooler
  • Graphics card (GPU)
  • RAM
  • Power Supply Unit (PSU)

Case

The computer case is nothing more than a fancy looking box that contains all the components of the PC. It can be opened and closed and normally, it has predefined areas with screws and holes where all other components are supposed to be placed and fixed.

Motherboard

The motherboard is a printed circuit to which all the other components of the computer are connected. From this plate the power is distributed to each of the parts of the PC. The motherboard has connectors for cables like power cables and data cables, slots for cards like GPUs, and sockets for CPUs.

Processor (CPU)

A CPU is the central processing unit of a computer, and without it, nothing really works. Almost everything you do on a computer will have to be calculated by the CPU in some way, so having a fast CPU (high clocks and a large number of cores) will make your PC faster.

Fan Cooler (CPU)

Everything that consumes energy also produces heat and the CPU produces a lot of heat. This means that it must be cooled down to work properly. What do we need to cool a CPU? A CPU cooler!

Graphics card (GPU)

Next is the graphics card. Its purpose is to compute anything that has to do with images and it sends these images (images, user interface, GUI) to the monitor.

Random Access Memory (RAM)

Random Access Memory (RAM). It's the temporary storage part of the computer's thinking if you compare it to a brain.

Power Supply Unit (PSU)

It is the part of the hardware that is responsible for providing energy to each and every one of the components of your computer.The important thing is to know how much energy your current PC will need to work in a stable manner and during a long period of time. as long as you need in the future if you plan to add more components, such as additional GPUs or disk drives.

Storage ( HDD / SSD / NVME )

Because RAM can't store anything without power and we want to be able to turn off our computer from time to time, we need a storage medium that will keep your data stored even when the power is off.

There are 3 main types of mass storage:

  • HDD
  • SSD
  • NVME

Hard Drive Disk (HDD)

Hard drives, also known as HDDs, are a computer component used to permanently store your data. This means that the data is not deleted when the unit is turned off as it happens in the data stored by the RAM memory. The first company to commercialize them was IBM in 1956.

They are made up of mechanical parts, which is why they are sometimes called mechanical hard drives, and use magnetism to record your data and files. It is made up of one or more hard drives joined by the same axis and that rotate at high speed inside a metal box. On each platter and on each of its faces, a read/write head reads or writes your data onto the disks.

The thinner the discs, the better the recording will be, and the faster they spin, the faster the data is transmitted, both when reading and writing. Typically, the speed of hard drives is usually 5400 or 7200 RPM (revolutions per minute), although some server-based drives can go up to 15,000 RPM.

In terms of size, mechanical hard drive enclosures can be 2.5" or 3.5".

Solid state drive (SSD)

Solid state drives or SSDs (Solid State Drive) are an alternative to hard drives. The big difference is that while hard drives use mechanical components that move, SSDs store files on microchips with interconnected flash memories. Therefore, we could almost consider them as an evolution of USB sticks.

SSDs usually use NAND-based flash memories, which, as they are also non-volatile, keep the information stored when the disk is disconnected. They do not have physical heads to record the data, instead they include an integrated processor to perform operations related to reading and writing data.

These processors, called controllers, are what make the "decisions" about how to store, retrieve, cache, and clean data on the drive, and their efficiency is one of the factors that determine the overall speed of the drive. Also, by not relying on a physical component to spin, it also makes for a quieter drive than mechanical drives.

Non Volatile Memory Express (NVME)

NVME (Non volatile memory express) is a new storage access and transport protocol for next-generation flash and solid-state drives (SSDs) that delivers the highest performance and fastest response times for all types of data loads. business work.

Monitor

The monitor is an electronic output device of the computer in which the images and texts generated by means of a graphic or video adapter of the computer are displayed. The term monitor usually refers to the video screen, and its main and only function is to allow the user to interact with the computer. A typical computer has a monitor with CRT (cathode ray tube) technology, the same used by televisions; however, today there is TFT (Thin Film Transistor) technology that significantly reduces the volume of monitors.

Mousee

is a pointing device used to facilitate the handling of a graphical environment on a computer. It is generally made of plastic, and is used with one of the hands. It detects its relative movement in two dimensions by the flat surface on which it rests, usually reflecting through a pointer, cursor or arrow on the monitor.

Keyboard

In computing, a keyboard is an input device or peripheral, partly inspired by the keyboard of typewriters, which uses a system of buttons or keys, to act as mechanical levers or electronic switches that send all the information to the computer. computer or mobile phone.
